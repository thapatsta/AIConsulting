A property management firm with 12 staff was manually extracting data from lease agreements, maintenance invoices, and supplier contracts — copying figures into spreadsheets, cross-referencing systems, filing PDFs. We automated the entire document pipeline. What used to take an hour now takes four minutes, and zero humans are involved.

SECTION 01

Twelve People Doing a Job That Machines Were Built For

The firm processed between 40 and 80 documents per week — lease renewals, contractor invoices, compliance certificates, insurance documents — each requiring a staff member to open it, manually read key data fields, and re-enter them into two separate systems. There was no standardisation, no automation, and no audit trail.

Errors were common. A transposed invoice number, a missed renewal date, a wrong figure copied across — each one required time to diagnose and correct, often only surfacing days later when a payment didn't match or a lease date was wrong. The team spent roughly 3 hours per person, per day, on document-related admin. Across 12 staff, that was 36 hours a day.

The real cost wasn't just time — it was attention. Every hour spent on manual data entry was an hour not spent on tenant relationships, property inspections, or strategic decisions. The work required enough concentration to be exhausting, but not enough judgement to be satisfying.

SECTION 02

Automated Extraction, Validation & Routing — End to End

We built a document intelligence pipeline that handles the full workflow: inbound document capture, field extraction, validation against source systems, exception flagging, and downstream data routing — without manual intervention for standard documents.

When a document arrives (via email, upload portal, or scan), the system classifies it by type, extracts the relevant data fields using a fine-tuned extraction model, validates the extracted values against the company's property management system, and routes the data to the correct destination. For standard documents, this happens in under 4 minutes — fully automatically.

The exception-handling layer means edge cases don't slip through unnoticed. Low-confidence extractions, mismatched values, or unfamiliar document formats are flagged for human review with the specific issue highlighted — so staff spend their attention only where it's actually needed.

"We used to joke that half our job was being a human OCR machine. Now we actually have time to manage properties instead of manage paperwork."
— Operations Manager, Property Management Firm
SECTION 03

How We Built It

Step 01

Catalogued Document Types

Worked with the team to map every document type they process — lease agreements, invoices, compliance certs, insurance docs — and defined the exact data fields needed from each.

Step 02

Built the Extraction Pipeline

Trained a document extraction model on 6 months of historical documents, then connected it to a validation layer that cross-references extracted data against existing records.

Step 03

Designed the Exception Flow

Built a human-in-the-loop review queue for low-confidence extractions — staff see exactly which field was uncertain and why, so reviews take seconds, not minutes.

Step 04

Integrated Downstream Systems

Connected the output to the property management platform and accounting system — validated data flows automatically to the correct record without any manual copy-paste.

Step 05

Deployed & Refined

Ran a parallel period where all extractions were verified before routing. Accuracy hit 97% within 3 weeks, at which point standard documents moved to fully automatic processing.

SECTION 04

From 36 Staff-Hours a Day to Under 2

Standard document processing time dropped from 45–90 minutes per document to under 4 minutes. Across the firm's weekly volume of 60 documents, that's 40+ hours of manual work eliminated every week.

Error rates dropped to near zero for standard documents. The exception queue — flagged for human review — handles less than 3% of volume, and those reviews average under 90 seconds because the system shows staff exactly what to check.

The 12 staff members affected now use the recovered time for higher-value work. The operations manager has since taken on two additional property portfolios without adding headcount. The system processes the increased document volume without any change to capacity.
