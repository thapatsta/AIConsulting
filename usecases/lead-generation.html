3× Pipeline Growth

01 The Challenge

A Pipeline That Dried Up Every Time Delivery Got Busy

The firm had no shortage of talent — three senior consultants and a small delivery team — but their business development process was entirely manual and entirely dependent on one person's energy on any given week. When client work was heavy, prospecting stopped. When prospecting stopped, the pipeline thinned. The cycle repeated itself every quarter.

Attempting to fix this with a part-time sales hire hadn't worked — the role required too much institutional knowledge to hand off easily. CRM adoption was inconsistent, lead data was scattered across spreadsheets and email threads, and follow-up sequences were built and forgotten. The team knew they had a problem but had no clear path to fixing it that didn't involve more people.

The real issue wasn't effort — it was that every step in their prospecting workflow required a human decision at a moment when the humans were already at capacity.

02 The Solution

Automated Prospecting, Enrichment & Outreach — Running 24/7

We built a three-layer automation that handles the full top-of-funnel workflow: targeted prospect discovery, contact enrichment, and personalised outreach sequences — without requiring any ongoing input from the team.

Each night, the system pulls a new batch of qualified prospects matching the firm's ideal client profile, enriches each contact with verified data, and slots them into a personalised email sequence timed to the firm's defined cadence. Replies and engagement events automatically update the CRM, trigger follow-up tasks, or escalate a lead to a consultant — all based on rules the team defined upfront.

The result: prospecting happens whether or not anyone on the team has the bandwidth to make it happen. For the first time, delivery and business development could run in parallel rather than competing for the same hours.

"We stopped treating prospecting as something we'd get to when things slowed down. It just… runs now. We walk in on Monday and there are qualified conversations already in motion."
— Managing Director, Regional Consulting Firm
03 The Process

How We Built It

01

Mapped the Ideal Client Profile

Worked with the team to define exact targeting criteria — industry, company size, role, geography, and buying signals — so the automation only surfaced relevant prospects.

02

Built the Prospect Discovery Engine

Integrated a data enrichment API with a nightly automation that sources, validates, and deduplicates new contacts against the existing CRM.

03

Designed the Outreach Sequences

Wrote and configured 3-touch personalised email sequences for each client segment, with conditional logic that adapts follow-up timing based on open/click behaviour.

04

Connected CRM & Escalation Logic

Automated reply classification and routing — interested replies are flagged and assigned to a consultant within minutes; unsubscribes are handled instantly without manual review.

05

Deployed & Handed Off

Ran a 2-week parallel test alongside the manual process, dialled in targeting and sequence copy, then transferred ownership to the client with a simple monitoring dashboard.

04 The Results

A Pipeline That Runs Independently of the Team's Bandwidth

Within 60 days of go-live, the firm had 3× the number of active conversations in their pipeline compared to their previous quarterly average — all without the team increasing the hours they spent on BD. The automation was surfacing 40–60 qualified prospects per week and converting roughly 8% to active email conversations.

More meaningfully, the 14 hours per week previously spent on manual list-building, data entry, and outreach scheduling were redistributed to client delivery and proposal work. The team no longer experienced the feast-or-famine cycle that had defined their business development for three years.

The system continues to run and improve. Monthly reviews of sequence performance have led to two rounds of copy refinements that increased reply rates by a further 22%. The foundation is in place to layer in LinkedIn outreach and event-triggered sequences as the firm scales.
